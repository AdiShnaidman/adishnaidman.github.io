I’m a Machine Learning Researcher focused on advancing the robustness, safety, and reliability of large language models. At DeepKeep, I research and build real-time detection and mitigation strategies to protect LLMs against adversarial prompts, misuse, and unintended behaviors.

I stress-test models under adversarial, edge-case, and real-world conditions to uncover vulnerabilities and develop research-driven solutions that strengthen model performance, integrity, and safe deployment. I’m also involved in collaborative ML research initiatives with leading academic institutions.

Research

My work sits at the intersection of LLM safety, adversarial robustness, and behavioral reliability. I build evaluation and detection methods that help identify and mitigate failures in production settings—especially under adversarial pressure and real-world misuse patterns.

Research interests:

  • LLM safety and robustness; trustworthiness and behavioral reliability
  • Adversarial prompting and prompt injection detection/mitigation
  • Large-scale evaluation & benchmarking under realistic threat scenarios
  • Model over-generation (latency/cost failures) and misuse/failure analysis
  • Representation learning and mechanistic interpretability (as applicable to safety)
  • Inference-time control / activation steering
